# Motivation & Questions to Ask Reference

## Part 1: "Why This Company/Role?" Frameworks

### Question 1: "Why do you want to work at [Company]?"

**Framework: Company → Mission → You**
1. Start with something specific about the company (product, mission, culture, recent achievement)
2. Connect it to your values or interests
3. Tie it to how you want to contribute

**Example structure:**
> "What drew me to [Company] is [specific thing]. As someone who [your background/interest], I've been following [specific project/initiative]. I'm excited about the opportunity to [your contribution]."

**Do NOT say:**
- "Because it's a great company" (vague)
- "Because of the compensation/perks" (even if true)
- "Because I need a job" (desperate)

### Question 2: "Why this specific role?"

**Framework: Skills → Role → Growth**
1. Identify which of your skills directly apply to this role
2. Explain what about this specific role excites you (not just the company)
3. Show how it fits your career trajectory

**Key:** Demonstrate you understand what the role actually involves day-to-day, not just the title.

### Question 3: "How do you see yourself contributing?"

**Framework: Quick Win → Long-Term Impact**
1. Identify one area where you could contribute immediately (based on your strongest skill match)
2. Describe how you'd grow into larger contributions over time
3. Be specific — reference actual projects or initiatives from the JD or company research

### Question 4: "What attracts you to our industry/product?"

**Framework: Personal Connection → Knowledge → Enthusiasm**
1. Share a genuine personal connection to the industry or product
2. Demonstrate knowledge — mention specific features, competitors, or trends
3. Express authentic enthusiasm (not performative excitement)

**Key:** If you don't have a personal connection, be honest about what sparked your interest. Authenticity > performance.

### Question 5: "Where do you see yourself growing?"

**Framework: Learn → Contribute → Lead**
1. Short-term (6-12 months): What you want to learn and how you'll ramp up
2. Medium-term (1-2 years): How you'll be making meaningful contributions
3. Long-term (3-5 years): The direction you'd like to grow (technical depth, leadership, etc.)

**Do NOT say:**
- "In your seat" (arrogant)
- "I don't know" (shows no ambition)
- Anything about leaving ("I want to start my own company")

## Connecting Background to Company Authentically

### For Students with Relevant Experience
- Reference specific projects, coursework, or research that directly relates
- Use concrete examples: "In my NLP research, I worked on [X], which is similar to how [Company] approaches [Y]"

### For Students Without Direct Experience
- Focus on transferable skills and genuine curiosity
- Be honest: "While I haven't worked directly in [domain], my experience with [related thing] taught me [relevant skill]"
- Show you've done your homework about the company

### For Career Changers
- Acknowledge the transition honestly
- Highlight what your unique background brings that traditional candidates don't
- Show deliberate steps you've taken toward this career (courses, projects, networking)

## Part 2: Questions to Ask Interviewers

### Category 1: Role & Technical (2-3 questions)

1. **"What does a typical day or week look like for someone in this role?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Shows you care about the reality of the work, not just the title
   - *Signal:* Practical, grounded

2. **"What's the tech stack for this team, and how do you make decisions about adopting new tools?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Shows technical curiosity and awareness that tools evolve
   - *Signal:* Engaged engineer, not just a task-doer

3. **"What's the most impactful project this team shipped recently?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Gets the interviewer talking about something they're proud of (builds rapport) and gives you insight into what "impact" means here
   - *Signal:* You care about meaningful work

### Category 2: Team & Culture (2-3 questions)

4. **"How does the team handle code reviews / peer feedback?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Reveals collaboration culture, quality standards, and how they invest in growth
   - *Signal:* You value quality and learning

5. **"What does onboarding look like for new team members?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Shows you're thinking practically about ramping up and contributing quickly
   - *Signal:* Self-aware, proactive

6. **"How would you describe the team's working style? More independent or collaborative?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Helps you assess fit and shows you're thoughtful about working dynamics
   - *Signal:* Mature, self-aware

### Category 3: Growth & Development (2-3 questions)

7. **"What opportunities are there for learning and development — conferences, courses, mentorship?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Shows you plan to grow, which signals retention
   - *Signal:* Growth mindset, long-term thinking

8. **"How does the team approach career development? Are there regular check-ins about growth?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Reveals whether they invest in people or just use them
   - *Signal:* You take your career seriously

9. **"What skills or traits have made someone particularly successful in this role?"**
   - *Why it's strategic:* Gives you a success blueprint AND shows you're oriented toward excellence
   - *Signal:* Ambitious, coachable

### Category 4: Strategy & Vision (2-3 questions)

10. **"What are the biggest challenges the team is facing right now?"**
    - *Why it's strategic:* Shows you're ready to tackle real problems, not just cruise
    - *Signal:* Problem-solver, realistic

11. **"Where do you see this team or product heading in the next year?"**
    - *Why it's strategic:* Helps you assess stability and direction
    - *Signal:* Forward-thinking, invested in the bigger picture

12. **"What's something about working here that you didn't expect when you joined?"**
    - *Why it's strategic:* Personal question that builds rapport and reveals insider perspective
    - *Signal:* Genuine curiosity about the human side

### Questions to Avoid

| Don't Ask | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "What does your company do?" | Shows zero research |
| "How much does it pay?" (first round) | Premature — save for offer stage |
| "How soon can I get promoted?" | Sounds entitled |
| "Do you check if employees are working?" | Signals trust issues |
| "I read on Glassdoor that..." | Puts interviewer on the defensive |
| "Can I work from home every day?" | Save logistics for later rounds |
| Questions you could Google | Wastes their time and yours |
