# Behavioral Interview Reference

## The STAR Framework

Every behavioral answer should follow this structure:

| Component | What It Covers | Target Length |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| **Situation** | Set the scene — where, when, what was the context | 1-2 sentences |
| **Task** | What was your specific responsibility or challenge | 1 sentence |
| **Action** | What YOU did (not the team) — specific steps, decisions, tools | 3-5 sentences |
| **Result** | Quantified outcome — what changed, what you learned | 1-2 sentences |

**Total answer length:** 60-90 seconds when spoken. Practice timing.

### Common Mistakes
- **Too much Situation, not enough Action** — interviewers care about what YOU did
- **"We" instead of "I"** — be specific about your individual contribution
- **No result** — always end with a measurable outcome or lesson learned
- **Fabricated stories** — interviewers can tell. Use real experiences, even if from academic projects

## Core Behavioral Questions by Competency

### Leadership
1. "Tell me about a time you led a team or project."
   - **Tests:** Initiative, delegation, accountability
   - **Good sources:** Group projects, club leadership, research team coordination, hackathon teams
   - **Follow-up likely:** "What would you do differently?"

2. "Describe a situation where you had to take initiative without being asked."
   - **Tests:** Proactivity, ownership
   - **Good sources:** Identifying a bug/improvement independently, starting a study group, automating a process

### Problem-Solving
3. "Tell me about the most challenging technical problem you've solved."
   - **Tests:** Analytical thinking, persistence, methodology
   - **Good sources:** Debugging sessions, algorithm challenges, research obstacles
   - **Key:** Walk through your thought process, not just the solution

4. "Describe a time you had to learn something new quickly to complete a task."
   - **Tests:** Adaptability, learning speed, resourcefulness
   - **Good sources:** Picking up a new framework for a project, learning a domain for research

### Teamwork
5. "Tell me about a time you worked with someone who was difficult to work with."
   - **Tests:** Interpersonal skills, professionalism, empathy
   - **Key:** Never badmouth the other person. Focus on what you did to make it work
   - **Follow-up likely:** "How did you resolve the conflict?"

6. "Describe your role in a successful team project."
   - **Tests:** Collaboration, communication, role clarity
   - **Key:** Be specific about YOUR contribution while acknowledging the team

### Conflict Resolution
7. "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate or supervisor."
   - **Tests:** Professionalism, communication, compromise
   - **Key:** Show you can disagree respectfully and find common ground
   - **Never say:** "I was right and they were wrong"

8. "Describe a time a project didn't go as planned. What did you do?"
   - **Tests:** Resilience, adaptability, accountability
   - **Key:** Own the setback, explain what you learned, show how you recovered

### Adaptability
9. "Tell me about a time you had to manage multiple priorities or tight deadlines."
   - **Tests:** Time management, prioritization, stress handling
   - **Good sources:** Balancing coursework with research/internship, finals week, project deadlines

10. "Describe a situation where requirements changed midway through a project."
    - **Tests:** Flexibility, communication, problem-solving under uncertainty
    - **Good sources:** Capstone projects, research pivots, shifting client requirements

## Mapping Academic Experience to Behavioral Answers

Students often think they don't have "real" experience. Map these:

| Academic Experience | Maps To |
|---|---|
| Group project where you divided work | Leadership, delegation, project management |
| Debugging a complex assignment | Problem-solving, persistence, technical depth |
| Teaching assistant or tutoring | Communication, mentorship, explaining complex topics |
| Research project pivot | Adaptability, handling ambiguity |
| Hackathon under time pressure | Working under constraints, rapid learning |
| Disagreement in group project | Conflict resolution, compromise |
| Managing coursework + extracurriculars | Time management, prioritization |
| Open source contribution | Initiative, collaboration, code review |

## Answer Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a behavioral answer, verify:

- [ ] Specific situation, not hypothetical ("I would..." is wrong — "I did..." is right)
- [ ] Clear individual contribution (not hidden behind "we")
- [ ] Quantified result where possible (time saved, grade improved, users affected)
- [ ] Under 90 seconds when spoken
- [ ] Ends on a positive note (result or lesson learned)
- [ ] Honest — could survive a follow-up question
