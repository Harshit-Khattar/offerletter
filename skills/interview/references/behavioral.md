# Behavioral Interview Reference

## STAR Framework

Situation (1-2 sentences) → Task (1 sentence) → Action — what YOU did, not the team (3-5 sentences) → Result — quantified outcome or lesson (1-2 sentences). Target 60-90 seconds spoken.

## Key Questions by Competency

**Leadership**: "Tell me about a time you led a team/project." Sources: group projects, clubs, hackathon teams.
**Problem-solving**: "Most challenging technical problem you solved?" Walk through thought process, not just solution.
**Teamwork**: "Time you worked with someone difficult?" Never badmouth — focus on what you did to make it work.
**Conflict**: "Time you disagreed with a teammate?" Show respectful disagreement and finding common ground.
**Adaptability**: "Multiple priorities or tight deadlines?" "Requirements changed midway?" Time management, pivoting.

## Academic → Behavioral Mapping

Group project → leadership, delegation | Debugging → problem-solving, persistence | TA/tutoring → communication, mentoring | Research pivot → adaptability | Hackathon → working under constraints | Open source → initiative, collaboration

## Checklist

- Specific situation, not hypothetical ("I did" not "I would")
- Clear individual contribution (not hidden behind "we")
- Quantified result where possible
- Under 90 seconds spoken
- Honest — survives follow-up questions
