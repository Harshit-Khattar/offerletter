# Career Self-Assessment Questionnaire

A structured questionnaire to help students clarify their career goals and priorities. Ask these questions one at a time, follow up on interesting answers, and use multiple choice when possible.

## Section 1: Technical Interests & Work Type

**Q1: What type of work energizes you most?**
- a) Building products and features (front-end, back-end, full-stack)
- b) Analyzing data and finding insights (data science, analytics, BI)
- c) Researching and experimenting (ML research, AI, R&D)
- d) Designing systems and architecture (infrastructure, platform, DevOps)
- e) Working with people and processes (product management, technical PM)
- f) Not sure yet — I like a mix

**Q2: Which of these activities do you enjoy most?**
- a) Writing code to solve a specific problem
- b) Exploring a dataset and discovering patterns
- c) Reading papers and implementing new techniques
- d) Optimizing systems for performance or reliability
- e) Collaborating with non-technical stakeholders
- f) Teaching or mentoring others

**Q3: What technologies or tools do you most enjoy working with?**
(Open-ended — let them list languages, frameworks, tools they gravitate toward)

**Follow-up:** "Is that because you like the tools themselves, or because of what you build with them?"

## Section 2: Industry & Domain Preferences

**Q4: Which industries interest you?**
- a) Tech (pure software companies)
- b) Finance / FinTech
- c) Healthcare / BioTech
- d) AI / ML companies
- e) E-commerce / Retail
- f) Social impact / Non-profit
- g) Government / Defense
- h) Media / Entertainment
- i) Education / EdTech
- j) Open to anything

**Q5: Does the company's mission matter to you?**
- a) Very much — I want my work to make a visible positive impact
- b) Somewhat — I'd like it to align with my values, but it's not the top priority
- c) Not really — I care more about the work itself and growth opportunities

## Section 3: Work Environment & Culture

**Q6: What kind of work environment do you prefer?**
- a) Fast-paced startup (wear many hats, less structure, high risk/reward)
- b) Mid-size growth company (some structure, still agile, growing fast)
- c) Large established company (clear career paths, stability, resources)
- d) Research lab or academic setting (deep focus, publishing, fewer deadlines)
- e) No strong preference

**Q7: How important is work-life balance to you?**
- a) Very — I want clear boundaries between work and personal time
- b) Moderate — I'm willing to put in extra hours during crunch times but not all the time
- c) Low priority — I'm at a stage where I want to go all-in and learn as much as possible

**Q8: Remote, hybrid, or in-office?**
- a) Fully remote
- b) Hybrid (2-3 days in office)
- c) In-office (I like being around people)
- d) No preference

**Q9: Team size and dynamics?**
- a) Small team (3-5 people, close collaboration, high ownership)
- b) Medium team (8-15 people, some specialization, regular standups)
- c) Large team (20+ people, well-defined roles, process-driven)
- d) I'd prefer to work mostly independently

## Section 4: Career Goals

**Q10: What are your short-term goals (next 1-2 years)?**
(Open-ended — let them think out loud)

Prompt if they're stuck:
- "Do you want to specialize deeply in one area, or explore broadly?"
- "Are you more focused on skill-building or title progression?"

**Q11: Where do you see yourself in 5 years?**
- a) Senior individual contributor (deep technical expert)
- b) Technical lead / architect
- c) Engineering manager / people leader
- d) Founder / entrepreneur
- e) Researcher / PhD
- f) Honestly, I have no idea — and that's okay

**Q12: What does career success look like to you?**
(Open-ended — this reveals what really matters to them)

Prompt if they're stuck:
- "Is it about impact? Compensation? Freedom? Learning? Recognition? Status?"

## Section 5: Practical Priorities

**Q13: Rank these in order of importance (1 = most important):**
- Compensation (salary + equity)
- Learning and growth opportunities
- Work-life balance
- Company brand / prestige
- Mission and impact
- Location / remote flexibility
- Team quality and mentorship

**Q14: Location preferences?**
- a) I need to stay in [specific area] (family, visa, personal reasons)
- b) I'm flexible within [country/region]
- c) I'm open to relocating anywhere
- d) I only want remote positions

**Q15: Compensation expectations?**
(Be direct — students often don't know what to expect)
- "What's your target total compensation? It's okay if you're not sure — I can share typical ranges for your target roles."

## Synthesis Template

After completing the questionnaire, synthesize the results:

```markdown
# Career Self-Assessment Results

## Profile Summary
[3-4 sentences capturing who this person is and what drives them]

## Key Themes
1. [Theme 1] — [supporting evidence from their answers]
2. [Theme 2] — [supporting evidence]
3. [Theme 3] — [supporting evidence]

## Priorities (ranked)
1. [Most important factor]
2. ...
3. ...

## Potential Tensions
- [If any answers seem to conflict, note them honestly]
- Example: "You value work-life balance highly but are drawn to startups — this is doable but requires careful company selection"

## Recommendations
- [How these results should inform their job search]
- [What to prioritize when evaluating opportunities]
- [Suggested next step: role clarity or target companies]
```

## Rules

- **One question at a time** — never dump the whole questionnaire
- **Follow up on surprising answers** — "That's interesting — tell me more about why you feel that way"
- **Skip questions they've already answered** — if earlier conversation covered a topic, don't re-ask
- **No judgment** — "I want a high salary" is a valid answer, not something to correct
- **Adapt the phrasing** — these are templates, not scripts. Match the user's level of formality
