# Cover Letter Review Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate a cover letter draft. For each criterion, provide specific feedback — not just "good" or "needs work."

## 1. Opening Paragraph

| Criterion | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Hook** | Does the first sentence grab attention? Is it specific to this company/role, or could it be sent to any employer? |
| **Company specificity** | Does it reference something concrete — a product, mission, recent news, or project? |
| **Role connection** | Does it clearly state what role they're applying for and why? |
| **No cliches** | Reject: "I am writing to express my interest in...", "I am excited to apply for...", "I believe I would be a great fit..." |

**Red flags:** Generic opening that could apply to any company. Starting with "Dear Hiring Manager" when the hiring manager's name is findable.

## 2. Body Paragraphs

| Criterion | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Evidence-based claims** | Does every claim have a specific example backing it up? "I have strong leadership skills" needs proof |
| **Quantified achievements** | Are there numbers, percentages, or concrete outcomes? At least 1-2 quantified results |
| **Relevance to JD** | Do the examples directly connect to the job description's requirements? |
| **Show don't tell** | Does it demonstrate skills through stories/examples rather than listing adjectives? |
| **Flow and transitions** | Do paragraphs connect logically? Does each paragraph have a clear purpose? |
| **2-3 key qualifications** | Is it focused on 2-3 strongest matches, or trying to cover everything? |

**Red flags:** Restating resume bullets verbatim. Listing skills without context. More than 3 main points (unfocused).

## 3. Company Knowledge

| Criterion | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Genuine understanding** | Does the letter show real knowledge of what the company does, not just surface-level facts? |
| **Specific references** | Does it mention specific products, projects, values, or initiatives? |
| **Connection to applicant** | Is the company knowledge tied to why the applicant wants to work there, or just dropped in to impress? |
| **Current and accurate** | Is the information up to date? No referencing outdated products or leadership |

**Red flags:** Vague statements like "I admire your company's innovation." Incorrect facts about the company.

## 4. Closing Paragraph

| Criterion | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Confidence** | Does it express genuine enthusiasm without desperation or arrogance? |
| **Call to action** | Is there a clear, professional next step? ("I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss...") |
| **Forward-looking** | Does it hint at what the applicant would contribute, not just what they'd gain? |
| **Clean sign-off** | Professional closing (Sincerely, Best regards) — not overly casual or stiff |

**Red flags:** "I hope to hear from you" (passive). "I am confident I am the perfect candidate" (arrogant). Repeating the opening paragraph's content.

## 5. Tone and Voice

| Criterion | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Authenticity** | Does it sound like a real person wrote it, or does it read as AI-generated? |
| **Consistency** | Is the tone consistent throughout? No sudden shifts from casual to formal |
| **Personality** | Does the applicant's personality come through? |
| **Professional but warm** | Is it professional without being stiff? Personable without being too casual? |
| **Style match** | If reference cover letters exist, does this match the user's natural writing style? |

**Red flags:** Every sentence starting with "I." Overly formal language that doesn't match the user's other writing. Buzzword-heavy paragraphs.

## 6. Structure and Length

| Criterion | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Under 400 words** | Recruiters skim. Every sentence must earn its place |
| **3-4 paragraphs** | Opening, 1-2 body paragraphs, closing. No more |
| **Paragraph length** | No paragraph longer than 5-6 sentences. Aim for 3-4 |
| **White space** | Is it visually scannable? Dense blocks of text get skipped |
| **Format consistency** | Consistent with the user's reference cover letters (same structure, same greeting style) |

**Red flags:** Over 400 words. Single massive paragraph. More than 4 paragraphs.

## 7. Complementary to Resume

| Criterion | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Adds context** | Does the cover letter provide context, motivation, or narrative that the resume can't? |
| **Not repetitive** | Does it avoid restating resume bullet points word-for-word? |
| **Fills gaps** | Does it address any potential concerns (career change, gap, lack of direct experience)? |
| **Different angle** | Does it present the applicant from a different angle than the resume? |

**Red flags:** Reading like a prose version of the resume. Not mentioning anything that isn't already in the resume.

## 8. Grammar and Readability

| Criterion | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Error-free** | No typos, grammatical errors, or awkward phrasing |
| **Active voice** | Primarily active voice, not passive ("I built X" not "X was built by me") |
| **Varied sentence structure** | Mix of sentence lengths. Not all starting with "I" |
| **Clear and concise** | No unnecessary words. Each sentence adds value |
| **Correct company/role names** | Company name spelled correctly. Role title matches the JD exactly |

**Red flags:** Company name misspelled (instant rejection). Passive voice throughout. Run-on sentences.

## Scoring Summary

After reviewing, provide:

1. **Strongest section** — what's working well
2. **Weakest section** — what needs the most improvement
3. **Top 3 specific changes** — actionable edits ranked by impact
4. **Overall readiness** — Ready to send / Needs minor edits / Needs significant revision
